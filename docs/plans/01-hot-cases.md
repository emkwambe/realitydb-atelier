## 1. The bet, restated

Every Monday at 8am ET, Atelier ships a free 30-minute module pulled
from the previous week's public business news. The crisis is real, the
dataset is synthetic, the deliverable is a Claude-graded briefing.
Atelier becomes the only platform on earth where last week's *Wall
Street Journal* front page becomes this week's training scenario.

**Why this is the keystone bet (per master roadmap §3):** it feeds
Atelier Rank, supplies the format for the Atelier Open tournament, and
generates the sample-size data that lets the Atelier Index exist.

**What success looks like in 90 days:**
- Twelve Hot Cases published, no Mondays missed.
- ≥2,500 net new email subscribers attributable to Hot Cases.
- ≥3 Hot Cases ranked top-10 on Google for a target keyword (e.g.,
  `<company name> <crisis> case study`).
- Zero legal demand letters received.

## 2. Why we research before writing the PRD

This is the bet most likely to get the company sued or
publicly embarrassed if we move fast and break things. The remaining
nine bets touch product surface; this one touches the press, the
legal system, and the reputations of named human beings.

Three categories of failure to avoid:

1. **Legal failure.** Defamation suit, trademark dispute, takedown.
2. **Editorial failure.** Sloppy sourcing turns Atelier into a
   tabloid; loses the credential's institutional credibility.
3. **Operational failure.** Friday-to-Monday cadence collapses on
   week 3 because the pipeline isn't real, and the brand promise
   ("every Monday") becomes a brand liability.

This plan documents the guardrails for each, and lists the research
that must complete before the PRD is even drafted.

## 3. The Hot Case Editorial Standard

This is the load-bearing artifact. Everything else (legal review,
production calendar, technical pipeline) follows from this. It exists
in this doc and must be re-signed at the start of each quarter.

### 3.1 Inclusion criteria

A real-world event qualifies as a Hot Case **only if all four of these
are true:**

1. **Already a public story.** Covered by ≥2 mainstream outlets
   (national newspaper, trade press, or wire service) at least 72
   hours before our publish date. We are never the breaking source.
2. **Structurally data-shaped.** The failure is something a query
   could have surfaced: hidden segmentation, broken cohort, off-policy
   transaction pattern, missed adverse event signal, supply-chain
   concentration, etc. If it's a leadership scandal with no underlying
   data trail (e.g., a CEO's offensive tweet), it's not a Hot Case.
3. **Settled in fact, not in court.** Either an SEC/regulatory filing
   has been made, or a public consent order, or the company has
   publicly acknowledged the issue. **Open litigation where facts are
   disputed is automatically out** — we wait for a filed complaint
   with documented facts, or we skip the week.
4. **The structural lesson generalizes.** A teachable pattern other
   companies could replicate the mistake on. One-off financial fraud
   by a single rogue actor is less interesting than "this is how
   escrow reconciliation breaks when third-party processors fragment."

### 3.2 Disqualifiers (any one of these = skip)

- Active criminal investigation with named, charged individuals.
- Recent layoff or restructuring where individual employees could be
  identified from our synthetic dataset.
- Events involving deaths or serious bodily harm — we do not turn
  human tragedy into a 30-minute training exercise.
- Sexual misconduct cases, child-safety failures, or anything where
  victim identity is reasonably traceable.
- Any matter where Atelier or Mpingo Systems has a commercial conflict
  (a customer, prospect, or competitor as subject).

### 3.3 Naming convention

For every Hot Case we publish one of three framings, chosen up front
in editorial review:

| Naming style | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Named subject** | Public SEC consent order, settled regulatory fact, established trade-press shorthand. | "Wells Fargo Account-Opening Pattern · 2016 (revisited)" |
| **Inspired-by** | Recent news, facts public but litigation ongoing. Dataset shape mirrors the real event but module title is generic. | "A Regional Bank's $3.1B Training Failure (inspired by recent enforcement)" |
| **Pure structural** | The lesson generalizes; naming the company adds nothing. | "When Third-Party Escrow Reconciliation Breaks" |

The footer of every Hot Case **always** includes:

> *Hot Cases are educational reconstructions. The dataset is fully
> synthetic and generated by RealityDB. No production data was used or
> obtained. Citations link to public primary sources only.*

### 3.4 Sourcing standard

Every Hot Case publish-page ships with:

- **≥3 primary sources** (SEC filings, court documents, regulatory
  press releases, peer-reviewed publications). Not three tweets, not
  three blog posts.
- **≥1 secondary source** (mainstream-press coverage that established
  the event in the public record).
- **A publication-date table** showing when each cited source went
  public. We never publish before the earliest mainstream coverage
  + 72 hours.
- **A "what we changed" note** explaining where our synthetic dataset
  departs from the real event (always: identities, exact dollar
  amounts within a band, time compression).

## 4. Guardrails

### 4.1 Legal / IP

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Defamation** (false statement of fact about a named company that causes harm) | Inclusion criterion §3.1.3 — only settled or publicly-filed facts. "Inspired-by" framing for anything within the 1-year statute window of fresh litigation. Every published page sent through a legal-review template (item §6.3). |
| **Trademark** (using a company name in module title) | Reference, not endorsement. Module titles use the company name only when the relationship is already vernacular ("Theranos blood-test case"). Never use logos. Never imply approval or affiliation. |
| **Trade libel** (false statements that disparage product quality) | We critique decision-making, never product quality unless quality is the *legal finding*. "TD Bank's training failed" — defensible because the OCC said so. "Bank X's product is bad" — not defensible. |
| **Fair use / commentary** | We rely on the educational-commentary doctrine in the U.S. (17 USC §107). Hot Cases must transform — they don't reproduce — the underlying reporting. Synthetic dataset is the transformation. |
| **Right-of-publicity** (named individuals) | **Never name individuals** unless they are public officials acting in official capacity (a named OCC examiner is fine; a named bank teller is not). Default: roles, never names. |
| **Jurisdictional risk** | Default audience is U.S./U.K. Avoid EU-specific subjects until we have an EU privacy-counsel review. Russia, China, sanctioned countries — never. |

### 4.2 Editorial

- **One editorial owner per Hot Case.** That person's initials sign
  the publish commit. Not "the team."
- **Bias check.** Every Hot Case names the *system* failure, not the
  villain. If the published draft reads as a takedown of a specific
  person, it does not ship.
- **Sources before story.** The narrative comes from the sources, not
  the other way around. We do not retrofit citations to fit a hot
  take.
- **No anonymous tips.** Even if a Hot Case is structurally accurate,
  if its only source is a private conversation with someone, we don't
  publish until that pattern shows up in public reporting.
- **Pull-down protocol.** Any Hot Case can be pulled from the public
  site within 24 hours of a credible legal demand. The page redirects
  to a transparent "this case was withdrawn pending review" note —
  not deleted, not denied.

### 4.3 Operational

- **The week never breaks the calendar.** If Friday's planned subject
  fails the inclusion criteria on Saturday, we **fall back** to one of
  three evergreen "case still hot" reserves prepared in advance. The
  Monday publish never slips.
- **Three reserve cases at all times.** The first three slots in the
  pipeline are always already-built. We are publishing from a buffer,
  not from this week's news cycle, when things are working right.
- **Single-author week limit.** Eddy may write at most 4 Hot Cases per
  quarter solo. The other 8 must have editorial/legal review by
  another named human being. Bus-factor mitigation.
- **Publication clock is in UTC.** Monday 12:00 UTC publish. East
  Coast still gets 8am ET on standard time; we don't shift the
  cadence when DST changes.

### 4.4 Technical

- **Synthetic data only, ever.** Hot Cases use the RealityDB engine
  to generate. We never load CSVs scraped from real sources, even
  public ones, even sanitized.
- **No real PII, ever.** Customer names, emails, addresses, account
  numbers in every Hot Case dataset are 100% engine-generated.
- **Audit log per Hot Case.** Each module ships with a published
  `manifest.json`: sources cited, engine version, pack hash, dataset
  hash, signing key ID. Anyone can verify the dataset was generated
  the way we say it was.
- **Pack and dataset are immutable post-publish.** Once a Hot Case
  ships, its pack and dataset are versioned and frozen. Updates ship
  as v2 with a visible changelog. We never edit history.
- **Free indefinitely.** Hot Cases stay free forever, even after they
  leave the front page. Revenue model is: Hot Cases drive email →
  email drives All-Access / Team sales.
- **No rank ladder writes for Hot Cases initially.** First 90 days,
  Hot Case submissions count toward a separate "Hot Case score" that
  the public profile shows but does *not* roll into Atelier Rank.
  This protects rank integrity while we calibrate difficulty. After
  90 days, decision point: roll in or keep separate.

### 4.5 Brand

- **Tone is analyst, not journalist.** Hot Cases analyze data, not
  motives. "The query you would run first" is the voice; "What were
  they thinking?" is not.
- **Visible expiration date.** Every Hot Case displays "Published
  2026-MM-DD" prominently. After 12 weeks, a "current best read"
  pointer appears at the top.
- **No clickbait.** Headlines are descriptive, not provocative. "TD
  Bank's $3.1B AML training failure" — fine. "How TD Bank ROBBED its
  shareholders" — never.
- **Cite the parent brand.** Every Hot Case publish page has a small
  "Generated by RealityDB · Curated by Atelier" line. Reinforces the
  engine = moat narrative.

## 5. Surface area

Six new surfaces are introduced by Hot Cases. Naming them here so the
PRD doesn't have to reinvent them.

| Surface | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `/hot-cases` | Index page. Latest 12, then archive by month. |

until the PRD is approved:
- `/hot-cases/archive/[year]/[month]`

  from main Atelier Rank — see §4.4)

Each task has a named owner and a deadline. Research output goes in

### 6.1 Legal — defamation safe-harbor research
- **Owner:** External counsel review (one-time, ~$1,500 budget).
- **Output:** A two-page memo covering: (a) educational-commentary
  protection under U.S. law; (b) anti-SLAPP coverage in our state of

  highest-risk subject categories we should pre-clear before
  publishing.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1 ships.

### 6.2 Legal — trademark + fair-use one-pager
- **Owner:** External counsel (combined with §6.1 review).
- **Output:** Internal cheat sheet for editorial decisions: when to
  name, when to "inspired-by", when to go pure structural. Lives in
  `docs/research/hot-cases/trademark-fairuse.md`.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1.

### 6.3 Legal-review template
- **Owner:** Eddy (drafts), counsel (approves).
- **Output:** A 12-question checklist run against every Hot Case
  draft before publish. Stored as a markdown template in
  `docs/templates/hot-case-review.md`.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1.

### 6.4 Operational — publishing pipeline RFC
- **Owner:** Eddy + lead eng (once hired).
- **Output:** RFC document covering: pack-build workflow,
  dataset-generation script, page-template scaffolding, manifest
  generation, email send pipeline (Resend? Postmark?), the three-case
  buffer mechanism, the pull-down protocol. Lives in
  `docs/research/hot-cases/pipeline-rfc.md`.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1.

### 6.5 SEO — keyword targeting research
- **Owner:** Eddy or contractor.
- **Output:** A live keyword sheet (Google Sheets or markdown table)
  ranking ~50 candidate Hot Case subjects by search volume + intent.
  Drives the editorial calendar prioritization.
- **Deadline:** Rolling; first 12 keywords picked before Hot Case #1.

### 6.6 Reserve case backlog
- **Owner:** Eddy.
- **Output:** Three fully-built reserve Hot Cases (pack + dataset +
  draft narrative) sitting in `content/hot-cases-reserves/`, never
  published, ready to deploy if a week's planned case fails inclusion.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1. **This is non-negotiable.** The
  Monday cadence cannot be promised without the buffer.

### 6.7 Pedagogy calibration — 30-minute briefing rubric
- **Owner:** Eddy + Claude API tuning.
- **Output:** A reduced 3-axis rubric (vs. the full 5-axis) tuned for
  the shorter 30-minute Hot Case format. Plus the prompt template
  used by `/api/grade-briefing` for Hot-Case-specific grading.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1.

### 6.8 Subscriber list pre-launch
- **Owner:** Eddy.
- **Output:** A working email list (≥250 hand-curated subscribers
  from existing network) primed to receive Hot Case #1 on launch
  Monday. Mailing-list provider chosen and integrated.
- **Deadline:** Before Hot Case #1.

## 7. Open questions for the team

These are decisions the founder + first hire must agree on before
PRD. They are not pre-decided here.

1. **Pricing.** Hot Cases are free forever (§4.4). Do we gate the
   *briefing-grading* step behind email signup, or behind a paid
   account? Recommendation: gate briefing-grading behind email
   signup only (free), but archive-access older than 12 weeks goes
   behind All-Access. Counter-argument: friction kills the funnel.
   **Decision needed.**

2. **Rank integration timing.** §4.4 proposes a separate "Hot Case
   score" for 90 days, then a decision. Do we instead integrate from
   day one with a heavy difficulty discount? Risk of separate score:
   it becomes a permanent second-class citizen. Risk of immediate
   integration: rank inflation. **Decision needed.**

3. **Pack source authoring.** Does Eddy write every pack, or do we
   commission domain experts (e.g., a former bank examiner) on a
   per-case fee ($500–$1500)? **Decision needed before week 4.**

4. **Geographic scope.** §4.1 defaults to U.S./U.K. subjects. Do we
   add EU after Q3? Asia? Counter: international subjects expand TAM
   but multiply legal-review complexity. **Decision needed Q4
   planning.**

5. **Co-branding.** If a sponsor (Snowflake, Stripe) wants to fund a
   Hot Case under their name, do we accept? Risk: editorial
   independence appearance. Mitigation: sponsored Hot Cases are
   labeled and never can be about a sponsor's competitor.
   **Decision needed before first inbound sponsor request.**

## 8. The PRD that comes next (outline)

Once §6 is complete and §7's questions answered, the PRD writes
itself in ~3 days. Outline:

- **§1 Goals and non-goals** — Reuse master roadmap §1; non-goals
  inherit from §6 of master roadmap.
- **§2 User stories** — Subscriber receives Monday email; learner
  opens module; learner submits briefing; recruiter clicks shared
  result; editor pulls down a case.
- **§3 Editorial workflow** — Friday research → Saturday legal review
  → Sunday pack build + dataset gen → Monday 12:00 UTC publish.
- **§4 Content schema** — `pack.json` extensions for Hot Cases,
  `manifest.json` shape, citation block format.
- **§5 Routing and UI** — Surfaces from §5 of this plan.
- **§6 Email pipeline** — Subscriber storage, send schedule, list
  hygiene, unsubscribe.
- **§7 Metrics** — Page views, email opens, briefing submissions,
  graded scores, rank impact (if integrated).
- **§8 Legal handling** — Legal-review template (§6.3) invocation,
  pull-down protocol, complaint inbox.
- **§9 Failure modes and recovery** — Buffer depletion, source
  retraction, technical outage on publish Monday, legal demand.
- **§10 Open issues** — Decisions deferred from §7 of this plan.

The TRD follows the PRD by ≤1 week and covers: dataset generation
script, page templates, email pipeline implementation, manifest
verification, archive scaffolding, search/SEO scaffolding, sitemap
update, deployment rollout (gradual: cron → Vercel cron, or GitHub
Actions to publish).
