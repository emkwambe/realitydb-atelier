## 1. Where we are today

By 2026-05-18 the platform ships:

- **Six full company modules** — NovaPay, MedCore, SupplyLink, TowerNet,
  ClearBank, OncoCare — each with 10 graded exercises, a Claude-graded
  CEO briefing, signed verifiable credential, two scenario variants,
  and citation trails to primary industry sources.
- **The data engine (RealityDB)** generating 5K-row enforced-narrative
  datasets at 97–99/100 quality.
- **Auth, progress, certificates, instructor-design doc**, and a
  marketing surface (landing + pricing + persona band + proof strip)
  using the tri-color brand system inherited from realitydb.dev.

The four structural moats (see `MARKETING-AND-BRAND.md`):

1. **The data engine** — narrative-enforced, citation-trailed, public
   quality scores.
2. **The deliverable** — Claude-graded 5-axis CEO briefing including an
   epistemic-honesty axis.
3. **Vertical depth** — six industries where mistakes cost millions or
   lives.
4. **The credential** — signed, publicly verifiable, names the specific
   crisis solved.

Every move in this roadmap amplifies at least one of those four. Bets
that don't are not on this roadmap.

## 3. Dependency map — why this order

Each arrow is a hard precedence, not a soft preference.

```
                     ┌─→ Bet 6 — Atelier Open
Bet 1 — Hot Cases ───┤   (open uses Hot-Case format,
       │             │    needs published cadence first)
       │             │
       └─→ Bet 2 — Atelier Rank
              │      (Hot Cases supply rank-eligible
              │       events; without them rank is
              │       stale between paid modules)
              │
              └─→ Bet 6 — Atelier Open
                  (tournament leaderboard *is* the
                   rank ladder under another name)

Bet 3 — Boardroom ───→ Bet 6 — Atelier Open
                      (anti-gaming: tournament
                       submissions must be defended
                       live or LLM ghost-writing wins)

Bet 7 — Co-Pilot ───→ everything
                    (drop-off is the silent killer
                     before any other bet shows ROI)

Bet 4 — BYOC ───→ Bet 8 — Pack Marketplace
                 (BYOC productizes pack creation;
                  the marketplace lets others sell
                  packs we generated for them)

Bet 9 — Open Engine ───→ Bet 8 — Pack Marketplace
                       (third-party authors need
                        public engine docs and an
                        Apache-licensed core)

Bet 10 — Atelier Index ←─── Bets 1 + 2 + 3
                          (the Index reports on data
                           generated by the first
                           three; can't ship until
                           N ≥ 500 graded briefings)
```

The two implications worth saying out loud:

1. **Bet 1 is the keystone.** It feeds rank (Bet 2), tournament format
   (Bet 6), and Atelier Index sample size (Bet 10). Slipping Bet 1
   slips three other bets.
2. **Bet 7 is the silent multiplier.** If module-drop-off stays above
   30%, every other bet sells fewer seats. Co-Pilot before everything
   except Hot Cases.

## 5. Quarterly OKRs — proposed

These are draft. Real OKRs get committed at the start of each quarter
with the team in the room.

### Q3 2026 — Foundation
- **O1: Establish a weekly cadence the world can rely on.**
  - KR1: Twelve consecutive Hot Cases shipped, no Mondays missed.
  - KR2: Email list goes from N₀ to N₀ + 2,500 net subscribers.
  - KR3: ≥3 Hot Cases ranked in Google top 10 for a target keyword.
- **O2: Convert credential into a career signal.**
  - KR1: Public Atelier Rank shipped with ≥500 ranked learners.
  - KR2: ≥20 learners enable "Open to opportunities" toggle.
  - KR3: First profile-page-driven inbound deal closed (any size).
- **O3: Cut module drop-off.**
  - KR1: Co-Pilot "Stuck?" button shipped in workbench.
  - KR2: Exercise-to-briefing completion rate ≥ 70% (baseline TBD).

### Q4 2026 — Engagement
- **O1: Make the briefing impossible to fake.**
  - KR1: Boardroom round shipped on every module.
  - KR2: ≥80% of graded briefings include a Boardroom transcript.
  - KR3: LLM-generated-briefing detection rate audited at ≥95% recall.
- **O2: Stage one moment the press cannot ignore.**
  - KR1: Atelier Open Q4 runs with ≥500 entrants and ≥1 named sponsor.
  - KR2: ≥3 trade-press writeups (TechCrunch, Bersin, Modern CFO, etc.).
- **O3: Publish the first Atelier Index.**
  - KR1: Index 1.0 ships in December with N ≥ 500.
  - KR2: ≥10 inbound media inquiries within 30 days of publication.

### Q1 2027 — Scale
- **O1: Productize the highest-ARPU motion.**
  - KR1: Three paid Bring-Your-Own-Crisis deals closed (any tier).
  - KR2: Median BYOC delivery time from intake to live module ≤ 21 days.
- **O2: Add one Disaster Library case the industry talks about.**
  - KR1: Wirecard module published with full primary-source bibliography.
  - KR2: ≥1 AACSB or compliance-curriculum committee adoption.

### Q2 2027 — Ecosystem
- **O1: Open the engine.**
  - KR1: realitydb-core 1.0.0 published under Apache 2.0.
  - KR2: ≥3 external citations in research papers or RFPs within 90 days.
- **O2: Open the marketplace, carefully.**
  - KR1: ≥6 third-party packs reviewed and approved.
  - KR2: First $10K in marketplace royalties paid out.

## 6. What we say no to

A short standing list of patterns to refuse, regardless of who asks.

1. **No mobile-native app.** It's a laptop activity.
2. **No live video instructor cohorts.** That is HBS Online; not our axis.
3. **No general-purpose chatbot tutor.** Bet 7 is the only chatbot
   surface; everything else is a Socratic intervention.
4. **No NFT / blockchain credential anchoring.** Signed-URL verification
   already does the job.
5. **No i18n before $1M ARR.** Translate when revenue forces it.
6. **No second AI provider abstraction layer until Claude 5.** Single-
   vendor risk is real; engineering cost of abstraction now is not
   justified.
7. **No reconstruction of victims' data in any module.** Disaster
   Library uses only structural patterns from public record.
8. **No paid placement of brand-name companies as "subjects" of modules
   without their counsel-signed consent.** Even satirical pieces
   need a line.

## 7. How to use this doc

- **Quarterly:** Re-read §5 before locking that quarter's OKRs. Edit
  the doc in place if the world has moved (recession, FTC ruling, new

  message.
- **Before any new bet:** Confirm it amplifies one of the four moats in
  §1. If it doesn't, the answer is no.
- **Before any contractor/hire:** Re-read §4. If the new role doesn't
  unblock a specific OKR, the answer is no.
- **When in doubt:** Re-read §6. The list of "no" is short and load-
  bearing.
